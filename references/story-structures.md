# Story Structures

## Launch Film Spine

Use this default structure for a 30-90 second premium SaaS launch video:

1. **World shift**: Name the change, pressure, or new expectation.
2. **Pain compression**: Show the current workflow becoming fragmented, slow, or opaque.
3. **Product entrance**: Introduce the product as the organizing principle.
4. **Capability proof**: Show 3-5 concrete capabilities through product-led moments.
5. **System effect**: Show how individual actions compound into team, company, or platform value.
6. **Trust beat**: Show reliability, control, security, integrations, or adoption.
7. **Hero resolve**: Land on the product, name, tagline, and confident final state.

The product entrance should not feel like a logo interruption. It should feel like the answer to the pressure established at the beginning.

## Narrative Frameworks

### From Chaos to Control

Use for operations, analytics, workflow, support, finance, infrastructure, and internal tools.

- Start with clutter, duplicated effort, or invisible status.
- Use motion to compress the mess into patterns.
- Introduce the product as the control surface.
- Show small actions creating large system clarity.
- End with a calm, organized command view.

### From Question to Answer

Use for AI, search, analytics, research, knowledge management, and business intelligence.

- Open with a hard question.
- Show scattered sources or ambiguous signals.
- Let the product gather, reason, rank, or synthesize.
- Reveal a specific answer with traceable support.
- End with confident action.

### From Manual to Automatic

Use for automation, agentic tools, integrations, and workflow builders.

- Show a repetitive human task.
- Identify the decision points.
- Let the product encode the rule or intent.
- Show a chain reaction where steps complete cleanly.
- End with the user supervising outcomes rather than doing every step.

### From Fragmented to Unified

Use for platforms, collaboration tools, ecosystem products, CRMs, and data infrastructure.

- Show separate tools, teams, files, or channels.
- Reveal friction at the boundaries.
- Introduce a shared object, workspace, graph, or source of truth.
- Merge streams into one product model.
- End with coordinated motion across the system.

### From Invisible to Visible

Use for observability, monitoring, analytics, compliance, and security.

- Start with a hidden condition or risk.
- Use scan, trace, reveal, or zoom to expose structure.
- Show the product turning ambiguity into readable states.
- Demonstrate a decision or intervention.
- End with continuous awareness.

### From Idea to Launch

Use for builder tools, design tools, developer platforms, marketing products, and creation workflows.

- Start with a blank, prompt, sketch, spec, or spark.
- Show structured assistance turning intent into artifacts.
- Show iteration and refinement.
- Show publishing, deployment, or distribution.
- End with the created thing alive in the world.

## Beat Allocation

For 30 seconds:

- 0-4s: Hook and world shift.
- 4-9s: Pain or opportunity.
- 9-13s: Product entrance.
- 13-24s: Three proof beats.
- 24-28s: System effect or trust.
- 28-30s: Brand resolve.

For 60 seconds:

- 0-6s: Hook and world shift.
- 6-14s: Pain compression.
- 14-20s: Product entrance.
- 20-42s: Four to six proof beats.
- 42-52s: System effect and trust.
- 52-60s: Hero resolve.

For 90 seconds:

- 0-8s: Hook and world shift.
- 8-20s: Pain, stakes, and audience identification.
- 20-28s: Product entrance.
- 28-62s: Capability chapters.
- 62-78s: System effect, trust, integrations, proof.
- 78-90s: Brand resolve and call to action.

## Sequencing Rules

- Begin with tension or desire, not a feature list.
- Introduce the product only after the viewer understands why it matters.
- Alternate abstraction and product evidence.
- Build from simple to complex.
- Give the most important reveal a cleaner, longer hold than surrounding beats.
- End on a composed state, not a busy montage.

## Copy Strategy

Use less copy than the first draft wants.

- Headline copy should name the idea, not describe every feature.
- Supporting copy should be short enough to read during motion.
- Product UI labels can carry detail while hero text carries meaning.
- Avoid stacking voiceover, large type, dense UI, and fast motion at the same time.
