# Product Insight Selection

## Purpose

Premium motion starts before visuals. Select the right product truths, claims, proof moments, and data details before building shots.

## Product Truth Extraction

Capture:

- Audience: who the video must persuade.
- Pain: what is slow, fragmented, risky, opaque, or expensive today.
- Promise: what changes after the product exists.
- Mechanism: how the product actually creates that change.
- Proof: what specific UI behavior, metric, workflow, or artifact demonstrates the mechanism.
- Emotion: how the viewer should feel: clarity, speed, control, ambition, trust, relief, leverage.
- Conversion goal: demo request, launch awareness, investor confidence, user activation, enterprise trust.

Do not proceed with only category language like "AI-powered productivity." Translate it into a mechanism and proof moment.

## Claim Selection

Strong claims are:

- specific enough to visualize
- differentiated enough to remember
- provable through product behavior
- short enough to carry in motion
- connected to a real user outcome

Weak claims are:

- generic: "work smarter"
- inflated: "revolutionary platform"
- abstract without proof: "unlock intelligence"
- too broad for one video: "run your entire business"

## Feature Selection

Choose 3-5 feature proof beats for most launch videos. Select features that create a narrative chain:

- input
- organization
- action
- collaboration or control
- outcome

Prefer features that show different kinds of value. Do not choose five features that all animate as similar dashboard panels.

## Proof Moment Design

For each proof beat, define:

- Before state: what is unresolved.
- User or system action: what changes.
- Product behavior: what the product visibly does.
- After state: what is now clearer, faster, safer, or more powerful.
- Evidence: UI label, metric, artifact, status, source, or workflow result.

If a feature cannot be represented as a before-action-after, it may be a weak motion beat.

## Data Selection

Use data that supports the story:

- Metrics should be plausible and tied to product value.
- Chart types should match the insight.
- Labels should be readable and domain-specific.
- Empty or decorative data should be removed.
- If showing AI or analytics, include sources, confidence, citations, ranking, or traceability where trust matters.

Bad data looks perfect, random, or meaningless. Good data feels selected by a product team that understands the customer.

## Product Detail Hierarchy

Classify details:

- Hero detail: the one thing the viewer must notice.
- Proof detail: the UI state or value that makes the claim credible.
- Texture detail: secondary labels, metadata, avatars, timestamps, comments, rows.
- Noise: anything that distracts from the beat.

Keep hero and proof details. Reduce texture. Remove noise.

## Taste Filter

Reject a shot idea if:

- it could sell any SaaS product
- it hides the product behind generic abstraction
- it needs too much explanation
- it looks impressive but proves nothing
- it repeats a previous beat with different labels

Upgrade a shot idea by asking:

- What is the sharpest product truth here?
- What specific UI state proves it?
- What transformation should the viewer see?
- What detail would a real buyer recognize as credible?
