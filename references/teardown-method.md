# Teardown Method

## Goal

Use teardowns to convert reference videos into reusable taste principles. Do not copy shots. Extract structure, pacing, hierarchy, choreography, and finish.

## Process

1. Watch once for overall feeling.
2. Watch again and split into beats.
3. For each beat, record time range, purpose, focal object, motion, transition, and takeaway.
4. Identify recurring patterns.
5. Translate patterns into reusable rules.
6. Mark what is reference-specific and should not be copied.

## Beat Teardown Template

Use this format:

```text
Time:
Beat name:
Narrative purpose:
First read:
Second read:
Composition:
Primary motion:
Secondary motion:
Transition in:
Transition out:
Product truth:
Premium signal:
What to avoid copying:
Reusable principle:
```

## What to Look For

### Narrative

- What question or tension opens the film?
- When does the product enter?
- How are features grouped?
- Where does trust appear?
- What is the final memory?

### Composition

- How much negative space is used?
- Where does the product sit in frame?
- How are UI surfaces cropped?
- What is kept out of frame?
- When does the layout become dense?

### Motion

- What causes each movement?
- How is attention transferred?
- Where are the pauses?
- Which transitions preserve memory?
- Which movements repeat as motifs?

### Product Treatment

- How literal is the UI?
- What product details are preserved?
- What is simplified?
- How are states shown?
- How does the video prove usefulness?

### Finish

- How are shadows, edges, highlights, and grain handled?
- Is the color system restrained?
- Do objects settle cleanly?
- Does typography align with product geometry?

## Pattern Extraction

After teardown, write patterns as rules:

- Weak: "They use cool 3D cards."
- Strong: "When showing platform breadth, arrange product surfaces in depth, hold one readable hero panel, and use peripheral panels only as context."

- Weak: "The intro is slick."
- Strong: "Open with a high-contrast transformation that makes the category pain visible before naming the product."

## Reference Ethics

- Do not transcribe or store long copyrighted passages.
- Do not recreate a reference shot one-to-one.
- Use short notes, summaries, and original analysis.
- Keep links and metadata in the demo library.
