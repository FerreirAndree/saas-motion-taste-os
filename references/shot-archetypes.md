# Shot Archetypes

## Hero Object Reveal

Purpose: Introduce the product, core interface, or brand object as the organizing center.

Composition:

- One dominant product surface or symbolic object.
- Large negative space around the object.
- Minimal supporting detail.
- Background depth that frames without distracting.

Motion:

- Slow approach, fold-open, assemble, or light-on reveal.
- Secondary elements arrive after the hero is established.
- Hold long enough for recognition.

Use when the product needs to feel important, stable, and premium.

## Workflow Compression

Purpose: Show a messy process becoming simple.

Composition:

- Multiple small inputs, messages, tasks, files, or events.
- A visible compression path into one product action or status.

Motion:

- Inputs stagger in.
- Related items cluster.
- Duplicates fade or merge.
- A single output lands with more visual weight.

Use for productivity, ops, support, finance, and automation tools.

## Feature Proof Moment

Purpose: Prove one capability with a concrete before and after.

Composition:

- Feature UI is legible.
- Before state and after state are visible or implied.
- Highlight focuses exactly on the action.

Motion:

- User or system action triggers a visible state change.
- Use a short anticipation before the result.
- Resolve with a readable after state.

Use for product-led videos where credibility matters.

## Data Cascade

Purpose: Communicate scale, intelligence, or transformation of information.

Composition:

- Data streams, rows, cards, events, nodes, or charts.
- A clear directional flow.
- A final insight or decision object.

Motion:

- Incoming signals move faster than the resolved insight.
- Filtering, grouping, ranking, or synthesis is visible.
- The final output should be calm and readable.

Avoid abstract data rain unless it maps to a product idea.

## UI Flythrough

Purpose: Move through product surfaces while preserving spatial memory.

Composition:

- Connected surfaces arranged in 3D or layered 2D space.
- Each stop has one readable focal feature.

Motion:

- Camera moves only when revealing new context.
- Avoid spinning or drifting.
- Use depth changes to show hierarchy.
- Let UI settle before text-heavy frames.

Use when showing platform breadth, but keep each stop specific.

## System Map

Purpose: Show integrations, teams, data sources, or platform architecture.

Composition:

- Central product model with connected objects.
- Group by function, not random orbit.
- Use labels only where they clarify.

Motion:

- Connections draw in order of causality.
- Nodes activate as the narrative names them.
- The system should resolve into a simple shape.

Use for platforms, enterprise workflows, infrastructure, and ecosystem stories.

## Timeline Transformation

Purpose: Show progress, history, automation, or sequence.

Composition:

- Clear axis or path.
- Events have hierarchy and spacing.
- Current moment or outcome is visually emphasized.

Motion:

- Draw the path before adding events.
- Reveal events in reading order.
- Use scale or light for important milestones.

Use when time or process is central.

## Split-Screen Contrast

Purpose: Compare old vs new, manual vs automated, scattered vs unified.

Composition:

- Strong visual difference between sides.
- Keep the winning side calmer and more composed.

Motion:

- The improved side should not merely be brighter; it should behave better.
- Use transformation from the old state into the new when possible.

Use sparingly. Too many comparisons feel like ads from a template.

## Collaboration Handoff

Purpose: Show multiple users, teams, or roles working through a product.

Composition:

- Role markers, cursors, avatars, comments, ownership tags, approvals.
- Shared artifact at the center.

Motion:

- One user's action creates context for the next user.
- Use handoff motion, not simultaneous chaos.
- Resolve into shared state.

Use for team tools, approvals, design, docs, PM, CRM, and support.

## Trust and Control Beat

Purpose: Reduce perceived risk.

Composition:

- Permissions, audit trail, security boundary, approval, admin control, versioning, or reliability signal.
- Calm framing and slower motion.

Motion:

- Avoid aggressive cyber tropes.
- Use containment, verification, lock-in-place, traceability, or clean status confirmation.

Use near the latter third of a launch film.

## Brand Resolve

Purpose: Land the film with memory and confidence.

Composition:

- Logo or product name.
- Short tagline or call to action.
- One supporting product or brand object.

Motion:

- Final transition should feel inevitable from the previous shot.
- Hold the final mark.
- Avoid adding new information at the end.
