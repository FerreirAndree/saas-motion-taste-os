# Visual System

## Purpose

The visual system should make the product feel like it belongs to a coherent world. It should support product meaning before style.

## Color

Use color as information:

- Brand color: identity and hero moments.
- Accent color: active state, selection, or insight.
- Neutral system: UI readability and premium calm.
- Warning or success colors: semantic states only.

Avoid flooding every shot with the brand gradient. Premium color is often selective.

## Depth

Use depth to express hierarchy:

- foreground for current action
- midground for product surface
- background for context, data, or brand atmosphere

Depth should clarify, not obscure. Keep important UI on readable planes.

## Light

Use light for focus and activation:

- edge light to separate product surfaces
- soft glow for active intelligence or processing
- directional light to reveal object form
- local highlights for selected states

Avoid heavy neon unless the brand genuinely calls for it.

## Material

Choose materials based on product character:

- Clean glass: intelligence, visibility, modern control.
- Soft matte panels: calm productivity and enterprise trust.
- Crisp white UI: clarity, utility, product truth.
- Dark polished surfaces: premium, technical, focused.
- Paper-like surfaces: documentation, knowledge, collaboration.

Do not mix material languages without a reason.

## Texture

Texture should be subtle:

- light film grain to prevent sterile digital flatness
- tiny shadow noise for depth
- soft gradients for focus, not decoration
- low-contrast grid only when it supports system structure

Avoid decorative blobs, random particles, and noisy backgrounds that fight UI.

## Brand World

Define a small set of repeatable visual motifs:

- shape language
- corner radius logic
- line style
- grid behavior
- icon style
- highlight style
- camera distance
- transition mechanic

Repeat motifs enough that the video feels authored.

## Premium Constraints

- Limit major colors per shot.
- Make background less interesting than the subject.
- Keep shadows consistent with light direction.
- Use scale contrast deliberately.
- Keep corners, strokes, and spacing optically consistent.
- Do not place UI on visually busy backgrounds.
