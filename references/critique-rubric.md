# Critique Rubric

Score each category from 1 to 5. Then provide the 3 highest-impact fixes.

## 1. Product Truth

5: The video expresses a specific product promise and proves it through concrete product behavior.

3: The video communicates a category benefit but could belong to many products.

1: The video is mostly aesthetic and does not prove the product.

Questions:

- What does the viewer know the product can do?
- Which shot proves the core promise?
- Could this concept fit a competitor without changing much?

## 2. Narrative Spine

5: The sequence has clear tension, product entrance, proof, system effect, and resolve.

3: The sequence has good individual shots but weak progression.

1: The sequence feels like disconnected feature cards.

Questions:

- Why does each shot follow the previous one?
- Does the product appear as an answer?
- Is there a memorable ending?

## 3. Visual Hierarchy

5: Every frame has a clear focal path and readable composition.

3: Important objects are present but compete with surrounding detail.

1: The viewer does not know where to look.

Questions:

- What is the first read?
- What is the second read?
- Is anything moving that should stay quiet?

## 4. Motion Motivation

5: Motion explains causality, state change, continuity, or product value.

3: Motion is smooth but sometimes decorative.

1: Motion is arbitrary or template-like.

Questions:

- Why does this element move?
- What changes because it moves?
- Where does attention land after it moves?

## 5. Timing and Rhythm

5: The video has varied pacing, clean holds, and confident transitions.

3: The pacing is serviceable but flat or rushed.

1: Everything moves at the same speed or important ideas are unreadable.

Questions:

- Which reveal needs a longer hold?
- Which beat can be cut?
- Is the rhythm serving voiceover or copy?

## 6. Typography and Copy

5: Copy is concise, well-timed, and integrated into the frame.

3: Copy is understandable but overexplains or competes with UI.

1: Copy is generic, too dense, or poorly staged.

Questions:

- Can the viewer read it in time?
- Does the line say something specific?
- Is the type aligned with the product system?

## 7. Product UI Treatment

5: The UI feels credible, legible, and cinematically composed.

3: The UI is plausible but too flat, dense, or generic.

1: The UI looks fake, unreadable, or decorative.

Questions:

- Is the UI proof specific?
- Are irrelevant details removed?
- Are state changes visible?

## 8. Finish and Polish

5: Spacing, edges, shadows, easing, color, and transitions feel precise and consistent.

3: The direction is strong but finish is uneven.

1: The work feels rough, noisy, or template-driven.

Questions:

- Are there unresolved positions or awkward endpoints?
- Are shadows and depth consistent?
- Does the final frame feel designed?

## Output Format for Critiques

Use this structure:

1. Overall diagnosis in one paragraph.
2. Highest-impact findings, ordered by severity.
3. Specific fixes with replacement direction.
4. Optional revised shot sequence or timing table.
5. Final taste score across the rubric.
